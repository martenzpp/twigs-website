Background

Tahliah Debrett Barnett is an English-Jamaican and Spanish descent singer, songwriter, producer, dancer, and visual artist.

She was born on January 16, 1988, in Cheltenham, England.

Her early career began as a backup dancer for artists like Jessie J, Kylie Minogue, and Ed Sheeran before transitioning to music.

Her stage name "FKA twigs" stands for "Formerly Known AS twigs", a reference to an earlier stage name she had to modify due to another artist using "Twigs".

Awards

FKA twigs has garnered multiple nominations at the Brit Awards, including Best Female Solo Artist and Best British Album.

In 2020, she won Best British Solo Act at the NME Awards.

She has been nominated for a Grammy, specifically for Best Music Video in 2015 with her song "Cellophane".

She has frequently been featured in "Best Albums of the Year" lists by Pitchfork, The Guardian, Rolling Stone, and others.
